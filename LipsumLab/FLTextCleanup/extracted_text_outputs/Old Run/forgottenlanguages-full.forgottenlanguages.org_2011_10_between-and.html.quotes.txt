Around the time of Philomenos, in 547/48, the inhabitants of Aphrodite, a rural town in upper Egypt, wrote a petition to empress Theodora in matters of taxation which was signed by fifty-one tax-paying, relatively well-to- do priests, landowners, and professionals. Fourteen of these people were illiterates in Greek, and among them were two priests. Even priests of the church that, by and large, is based on the written word, were illiterates
