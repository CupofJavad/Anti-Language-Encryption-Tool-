The Cambrian probe gently landed on the designated landing site, at DENIED, in Mauritania. It started to probe the soil performing the programmed soil micromorphological elemental analysis. As modern humans depend physically and biologically on the controlled use of fire, the probe was programmed to perform analyses that would determine whether the planet was ever inhabited by modern humans or not. At that time, the landing site looked as an Aeolian dunescape (Rigel-2, Mizar-7, Sol-3 class). The magnetometry, thermoluminescence, andÂ  XRD analysis checked positive. So did the analysis of phytoliths. The probe concluded the site was used as a camp by modern humans and that the current findings related to controlled camp fire. The probe then reported that paleomagnetic and FTIR analysis checked negative, and discarded the site proceeding to next target of interest. We knew the probe was lying, and we knew the probe knew it was lying to us. Post-analysis of the mission showed which rules triggered such a behavior on the side of the probe: the algorithm responsible for hiding information that could entail a danger to other species
