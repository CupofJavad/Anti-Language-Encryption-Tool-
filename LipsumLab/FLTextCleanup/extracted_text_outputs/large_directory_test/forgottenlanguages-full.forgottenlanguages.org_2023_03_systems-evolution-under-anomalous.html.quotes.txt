There is likely a universal probabilistic law of evolutionary convergence that is inversely proportional to system's complexity; that is, the simpler a system is, the greater chances are that similar systems will be abundant throughout the Universe. The more complex a system is, the more rare convergence is likely to be. Complexity in systems is an integration of temporal evolution and probabilistic events. The longer a system is around, the greater chance it has to adapt through regular cycles and, at any given time, to mutate through stochastic events

Any dynamic environment needs reciprocity to solve a social dilemma. This compels communication to emerge among agents so that they can obtain a high payoff. That's how a communication evolves. In other words, pressure to cooperate leads to the evolution of communication

When a dilemma tempting an agent to exploit is relatively small, the population uses a single signal for its alarm call. However, with growing dilemma strength, reliability of the alarm call decays, because of an increase in the number of mimics (who use false signals). The social system begins to fluctuate between cooperative (reliable alarm call) and defective (false information) eras. This is a perturbation phase, commonly observed in various unsteady dynamic systems. Later, assuming a strong dilemma, the communication value of the alarm call is completely disrupted, leading to a situation called the Tower of Babel
