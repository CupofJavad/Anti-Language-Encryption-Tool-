The standard identification paradigm is this: if they are not ours, and they are theirs. With UAPs there is a paradigm-shift because the objects are fully capable of hiding themselves from our technological sight if they know we are looking for them, and particularly when they are looking for us. We need to take into consideration the approaches that will allow us to search for the UAP agent in its most likely form, that is, when neither we nor them are expecting to have an encounter. We call this strategy blind flight or, simply, wandering. It consists of releasing several MilOrbs to perform transects without assigning them any specific mission profile, looking for a chance encounter with the UAPs. The transects are totally random.
